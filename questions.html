Openness to Experience reflects the extent to which an individual is imaginative, curious, and open to new ideas and experiences. People high in openness are often creative and willing to explore novel concepts, while those low in openness may prefer routine and familiarity.

Neuroticism measures the tendency to experience negative emotions such as anxiety, depression, and moodiness. High neuroticism is associated with emotional instability and vulnerability to stress, whereas low neuroticism indicates a more calm and stable emotional state.

Conscientiousness refers to the degree of self-discipline, organization, and dependability an individual exhibits. High conscientiousness is associated with being thorough, reliable, and goal-oriented, while low conscientiousness may reflect a more spontaneous and less orderly approach to life.

Agreeableness measures how cooperative, compassionate, and friendly an individual is. High agreeableness is associated with being trusting, kind, and supportive, whereas low agreeableness might reflect a more competitive and less empathetic nature.

Extraversion assesses the extent to which an individual is outgoing, energetic, and assertive. High extraversion is linked to sociability, enthusiasm, and talkativeness, while low extraversion may indicate a more reserved and introverted disposition.
